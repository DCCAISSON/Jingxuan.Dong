I am a PhD candidate in Agricultural Economics and Management at Zhejiang University. I received my M.A. in Agricultural & Forestry Economics and Management from Hainan University in June 2024, and my B.A. in Logistics Management from Southeast University in June 2020.

My primary research interests include rural development, the digital economy, and human capital, with a focus on how infrastructure and technology impact agricultural productivity and rural livelihoods.

Working Papers

Highways, Market Inequality, and Agricultural Development: Evidence from China's Green Agricultural Product Certification

Supervisor as first author, Jingxuan Dong (Corresponding Author)

Under Review World Development SSCI Q1
Examines how transportation infrastructure affects market access disparities in green agricultural certification adoption, leveraging China's national certification database and highway network data.

Agricultural Industrial Agglomeration, Technology Spillover, and Smallholder Income Growth in the Context of Low-Altitude Economy

Jingxuan Dong & Gan Yu

Under Review China Economic Review SSCI Q1
Quantifies drone technology spillover effects on smallholder farming clusters using spatial econometric models and UAV operation data from China's 2589 counties.

Land Use Optimization and Food Security: Evidence from 10 Provinces in China

Supervisor as first author, Jingxuan Dong (Third Author)

Under Review China Land Science CSSCI
Integrates remote sensing data and crop modeling to optimize land allocation for food security under climate uncertainty.

Impact of Media Use and Ecological Policy Cognition on Farmers' Green Production Behavior

Supervisor as first author, Jingxuan Dong (Fourth Author)

Under Review Journal of Resources Science CSSCI
Analyzes media exposure effects on pro-environmental agricultural practices using structural equation modeling.
